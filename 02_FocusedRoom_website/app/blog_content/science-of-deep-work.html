âš¡ Common Deep Work Mistakes (And How to Fix Them)

Mistake #1: "I'll just check this one thing first"

Why it kills you: That "one thing" becomes 20 things. Your prefrontal cortex is now primed for shallow work, not deep work.

Fix: Start your deep work with 5 minutes of breathing or journaling. Give your brain a runway, not a cliff.

Mistake #2: Scheduling deep work when you're already drained

Why it kills you: Deep work requires cognitive resources. You can't do it on fumes.

Fix: Schedule deep work for your peak hours, not your leftover hours. Morning person? Do it first. Night owl? Protect your evening.

Mistake #3: No clear definition of "done"

Why it kills you: Without a clear goal, your brain will convince you that "just 10 more minutes" of research is necessary. It's not.

Fix: Before each deep work block, write down: "By the end of this session, I will have ___________." Be specific.

Mistake #4: Thinking you need a perfect setup

Why it kills you: You waste your peak hours "getting ready" instead of actually working.

Fix: Good enough is good enough. Start messy. Optimize later.
